About me...

Hey! I am a computer science and mathematics PhD student from Ireland. I am currently interning at the National Institute of Informatics Japan, and after completing my PhD funded by Science Foundation Ireland. Here, I was a member of the BDARG group. In my PhD, I initially focused on projects centred around statistical analysis, visualisation of networks, moving on to more applied projects involving data collection and processing, and statistical machine learning. Broadly speaking, I aim to tackle the issue that current AI systems lack the robustness, diversity and creativity of humans, and we have a poor understanding of them. One angle I have approached this is through mechanistic and concept-based interpretability, with the goal of understanding a model‚Äôs representations. I think it would be very cool to one day understand the rich internal structure of deep neural networks, or at least have better tools to reduce their the black-box mysterious nature. I am also interested in understanding and improving upon current model limitations such as robustness, quality and diversity, and open-endedness. My research spans both image models and LLMs, and I am open to studying multimodal architectures in the future.

I love to collaborate on interesting projects! I have been very fortunate to intern with MEDGIFT group in Switzerland as well as involve myself in various open science initiatives in my free time such as Aya led by Sara Hooker of CohereForAI and various fascinating projects with EleutherAI.
